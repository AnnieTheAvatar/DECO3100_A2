What's the damage?

Water Usage

Did you know that producing dairy milk requires a lot of water? Cows, just like us, need water to stay healthy and produce milk. They also need water for their metabolic functions. On top of that, the crops that cows eat, like grass and grains, also need water to grow. All this water adds up and can put a strain on our water resources, especially in areas where water is already scarce. So, it's important for us to be mindful of how much water goes into producing milk.

Land Usage

Another thing to think about is the amount of land needed for milk production. Cows need space to graze and eat grass, and that requires large pastures. Sometimes, forests and other natural habitats are cleared to make room for these pastures. This can harm the environment by contributing to deforestation and the loss of animal homes. Additionally, cows also eat crops like corn and wheat, which require land for cultivation. When more land is used for growing cow food, it can lead to more deforestation and the loss of biodiversity.
